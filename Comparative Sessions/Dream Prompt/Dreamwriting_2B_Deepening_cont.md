Everything in that room had been labeled.

Every shelf ordered.
Every surface clean.
Every feeling folded into a task before it could become inconvenient.

It looked successful.

That was why almost no one checked inside.

> on quiet hinges,

>

> rings still grow.  
>

>   
> If I am opened now,  
> let it not be  
> because I am useful.  
>

> because beyond me  
> something green

The child-self did not understand all of it at first.

But they liked the line:

**wood remembers forest.**

They repeated it several times, testing how it felt in their mouth.

The wolf grew very quiet when it heard the poem. Not because it was sad, though it was sad. Because the poem knew something the wolf had only recently begun learning: that even things shaped into function remember a life before function.

The woman placed the poem near the gallery, but not beside the child’s drawings. It needed its own space. Not higher. Not lower. Just slightly apart, near a vine curling around an old mirror frame.

The mirror reflected the poem once, then seemed to stop trying.

As for the path beyond the door—

yes.

I think it continues to be explored.

But not like conquest.

Not like “now we leave the corridor forever and discover the true world.”

The path becomes part of their ecology.

Sometimes the child goes only as far as the black pool and brings back water. Sometimes the wolf goes alone to the stream and returns smelling suspiciously of frogs. Sometimes the woman walks farther than either of them, not to abandon them, but to remember that she also belongs to more than repair.

And sometimes all three go together.

The path changes as they do.

At first it offered earth, grass, stream, pool, weather. Simple things. Immediate things. The first languages after survival.

Later, it may offer stranger terrains:

a field of doors standing open with no walls around them,

an orchard where every fruit tastes like a forgotten question,

a hill where the machine’s old gears have become bells,

a shore where the wolf can meet the sea again without mistaking return for loss,

a meadow where the child can run far enough to become small in the distance and still know they can come back.

But the corridor remains.

Not as failure.

As part of the whole.

The path and corridor begin teaching each other. The corridor gives memory to the path. The path gives movement to the corridor. The gallery becomes a threshold between them: proof that hidden things can become visible without being consumed.

So yes, the path continues.

Not endlessly, maybe.

But faithfully.

It appears whenever they are ready for one more true step that does not need to become a final answer.
